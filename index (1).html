What is Forex?

Everything you need to know about the world's largest financial market — explained simply.

What is Forex?

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the global market where currencies are traded. With over $7 trillion traded daily, it's the world's largest and most liquid financial market — open 24 hours a day, 5 days a week.

How Trading Works

Trade currencies in pairs — buy one while selling another. When the value of the currency you bought rises relative to the one you sold, you profit. It's that simple, yet incredibly powerful.

Currency Pairs

Currencies are always traded in pairs like EUR/USD, GBP/JPY, or AUD/NZD. The first currency is the base, the second is the quote. If EUR/USD = 1.0892, €1 = $1.0892.

Risk Management

The golden rule: never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. Use stop-loss orders, proper position sizing, and never trade with money you can't afford to lose.

Beginner Mistakes

Overtrading, ignoring risk management, trading on emotions, chasing losses, and not having a plan are the top mistakes new traders make. Learn to avoid them and you're already ahead.

Start With Small Money

You don't need thousands to start. Open an account with as little as $50-$100. Use a micro or cent account to practice real trading with minimal risk while you build your skills and confidence.
